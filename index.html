On the way to Autonomous Delivery

Our vision is an end to end autonomous vehicle designed specifically to handle item deliveries in urban environments. Current food delivery solutions depend on human couriers to achieve product fulfillment. If self driving vehicles were employed instead, delivery fees could be dramatically reduced. Our project focuses on the perception systems that would power such a vehicle.

What is Caravel?

One of the fastest growing markets in 2020 is food delivery. Especially in cities where traffic congestion is common and it is not feasible to own a car, people are finding it increasingly advantageous to use services such as InstaCart or Uber Eats to retrieve groceries or meals. Unfortunately, delivery fees are quite expensive. When getting takeout delivered, the cost of deliveries may be on par with the price of the original meal in extreme cases. If delivery fees could be reduced, then food delivery would be viable to a much wider range of people. One way to reduce delivery fees would be to employ autonomous vehicles instead of human drivers. The core motivation for this project is the concept of a compact, low-cost self driving vehicle designed specifically to carry groceries and takeout orders instead of passengers that could navigate the streets of an urban environment. As the vision for this project is quite demanding, the scope of our capstone has been reduced to only include select software components of an autonomous delivery vehicle. Specifically, the perception system is chosen as the primary subject of our capstone.
